# Artificial Intelligence and Multi-Agent Systems

> Course Code: AI-MA-101
> Level: Undergraduate / Graduate
> Estimated Duration: 48 hours (16 chapters × 3 hours)
> Prerequisites: Python programming, basic data structures

---

## Course Description

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to Artificial Intelligence with a focus on Large Language Models (LLMs) and Multi-Agent Systems. Students will learn the theoretical foundations of modern AI, gain hands-on experience with state-of-the-art tools, and build their own multi-agent collaboration system.

The course bridges traditional AI concepts with cutting-edge developments in generative AI, covering everything from classical machine learning to prompt engineering, Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and autonomous agent architectures. By the end, students will understand how to design, implement, and evaluate multi-agent systems for real-world applications.

---

## Learning Objectives

After completing this course, students will be able to:

1. **Explain** the evolution of AI from rule-based systems to modern foundation models
2. **Understand** the architecture and training paradigms of Large Language Models
3. **Master** prompt engineering techniques including chain-of-thought, few-shot, and structured prompting
4. **Build** Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems for knowledge-grounded applications
5. **Design** multi-agent architectures with role specialization and inter-agent communication
6. **Evaluate** agent performance using quantitative and qualitative metrics
7. **Implement** a complete multi-agent teaching system as the capstone project
